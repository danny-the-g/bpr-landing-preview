# Blueprint Reloaded: what we run on the daily calls

Five live group coaching calls a week, inside one private community. Every call opens by asking each person what they want to work on that day, then we run drills across six areas.

**1. Vocal projection**
Loud "eeeee" and full-volume work to unstifle the voice, so people carry a room instead of shrinking in it.

**2. Emotional range and tonality**
The same line run through apathy, grief, anger, and joy. Rapport tonality, deep self-hypnotic delivery, expressive control on command.

**3. Fast talking under pressure**
Non-stop yap while others interrupt and diss the speaker (calibrated 1 to 10 beforehand). People stay sharp and composed while being pushed.

**4. Sales practice**
Sell me something, usually a made-up or fantasy product to keep it fun. Rounds of 60 seconds, then 30, then 20, to force the pitch hard-hitting and to the point. Builds persuasion and getting to the message fast.

**5. Frame battles**
Two people go head to head, each holding their own frame while the other tries to knock them off it. Builds composure, presence, and the ability to stay grounded and unreactive under social pressure.

**6. Spontaneity and flow**
Free association, merge, yes-and improv, and storytelling out of the blue with emotion. People stop scripting and start flowing.

**7. Humour and wit**
Drills to sharpen comedic timing and land jokes on the spot: super expressive about something boring, super boring about something crazy, and playing off other people fast. People get funnier without forcing it.

**8. Killing self-consciousness**
Controlled roasting with a hard stop word, plus absurd noises that make caring what others think impossible. The goal is genuine non-neediness, not the fake "look, I don't care" front that reads through a thousand phones.

**9. Self-review loop**
Record across several days, listen back, run it again. Then stop for one to two weeks, come back, and watch the old recordings. That is when people finally see where they were forcing, faking, and try-harding.

Named drills in the rotation include magic 8-ball, the 5 game, shotgun, vocal stretch, free association, vocal cadence, range of expression, and vacuum.

Most of it maps to the standard program material. The real payoff shows up when members take it out and talk to people in the real world.
