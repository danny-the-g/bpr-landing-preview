# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Singers who already know they can't hit their ceiling alone: gigging vocalists, worship leaders, content creators, aspiring artists. They've tried YouTube tutorials and stalled, or took lessons that were too expensive or too infrequent to build real habit. They're scrolling on their phone, likely from a social post or DM, deciding in under a minute whether this is worth $47 a month. They convert on proof that people are actually training every day, not on polish.

## Product Purpose

Sell membership into Blueprint Reloaded (BPR), a $47/month vocal training community run entirely in WhatsApp. Members get 5 live group exercise calls a week (untangling drills, projection drills, free association, vocal stretch, and more added over time) plus daily community accountability, sweetened with 3 bonus digital products. The page's job: make the value stack undeniable against $47/month, and convert into a Stripe checkout that auto-delivers a WhatsApp invite.

## Brand Personality

Confident, current, premium-DTC energy, not underground gym-flyer energy (superseded an earlier darker direction after the founder shared reference pages: Western Rise, BlowLTD, Campaign Monitor, HomeLoanGurus). Full-bleed photo hero, clean white/light sections, bold single-accent CTA, icon-row trust signals, star-rating testimonials, numbered how-it-works. Still sells the room, not the credentials: the primary trigger is FOMO, "I need to be in this room," not certification-style trust badges. Directness and intensity now live in copy weight and color contrast, not in a dark gym-poster palette.

## Anti-references

Not a Teachable/Kajabi cream-and-blue course template. Not hustle-bro MLM energy (no countdown timers, no fake scarcity, no stock photos of people pointing at laptops). Not generic corporate SaaS gradient-text hero. No coach name or headshot is available yet; the page must work without personal-authority framing and instead sell the room/community and the training system itself. The hero photo slot is a placeholder until the founder supplies real photography, never a fabricated "coach" likeness.

## Design Principles

- Sell the room, not the resume. Proof comes from call cadence and community activity, not credentials.
- Specific over polished. Real drill names (untangling drills, projection drills, free association, vocal stretch) beat generic "vocal coaching" language.
- The value stack is the argument. The page's core persuasion mechanic is making $47 look absurd against what's included; every section should earn its place in that math.
- Placeholders must read as intentional, not broken. Bonus products and testimonials are marked as placeholders until real content lands, without looking unfinished to a visitor.
- Frictionless exit to WhatsApp. The entire funnel exists to get someone from "interested" to "in the group" in the fewest possible steps after payment.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum: body text contrast >= 4.5:1 even against a dark, intense palette. Respect prefers-reduced-motion. No motion-only cues for critical information (price, CTA). Keyboard-navigable CTA and FAQ accordion.
